Malala Yousafzai

She who wields a pen.

Malala Yousafzai is an activist for female education.
She grew up in Swat Valley with her family and continued to go to school against the rules of the Taliban.
Malala was later shot by the Taliban because of her actions, enraging the world, while also bring awareness to the issue.
She recived a Nobel Peace Prize for her bravery to stand up for girls' education, even if it meant endangering her own life.

  • Malala recieved a Nobel Peace Prize for her acomplishments and bravery.
  • Malala published a book titled "I am Malala" which quickly became a bestseller.
  • Malala spoke out for Syrian refugees and urged world leaders to fund their education to save the future of Syria.
  • Malala spoke out against the Taliban, risking her own safty for the future of other girls.
  • Malala continues to inspire the world today, showing that just one person and one pen could change the world.
